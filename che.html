Introduction

Water pollution, a pressing global issue, is underscored by alarming data and surveys. According to the World Health Organization, over 2 billion people lack access to safe drinking water due to contamination, leading to severe health implications. Industrial discharges contribute significantly, with a study by the United Nations revealing that 80% of global wastewater is discharged untreate. In India, water pollution emerges as a critical challenge, as reflected in compelling data and surveys. The Central Pollution Control Board reports that over 60% of India's water bodies face pollution, threatening both ecosystems and human health. A National Green Tribunal study indicates that untreated industrial effluents are a major contributor, adversely affecting rivers like the Ganges. Agricultural practices further exacerbate the issue, with excessive pesticide and fertilizer runoff. The NITI Aayog underscores the severity, revealing that 21 major Indian cities will run out of groundwater by 2020. These statistics underscore the imperative for robust policies and sustainable practices to address water pollution in the Indian context.

Causes of Water Pollution

  • Industrial Discharges
  • Agricultural Runoff
  • Waste Disposal
  • Oil Spills
  • Plastic Pollution

Effects of Water Pollution

Water pollution has severe consequences, including:

  • Loss of Biodiversity
  • Drinking Water Contamination
  • Health Issues
  • Disruption of Ecosystems

Preventing Water Pollution

Effective measures to prevent water pollution include:

  • Proper Waste Disposal
  • Reducing Plastic Usage
  • Conserving Water Resources
  • Water Treatment and Recycling
